Annotated Bibliography

“Public Transportation Facts - APTA.” APTA, www.apta.com/news-research/about-the-industry/public-transportation-facts/. Accessed 28 May 2026.

 APTA concisely lists a lot of information and statistics surrounding the benefits of using public transportation over cars. This includes usage statistics, economic opportunities, safety, money, gasoline consumption, carbon footprint, and personal opportunities. They highlight the benefits because they believe that public transportation is something that should be supported within our society and especially within America. I believe this source is useful for the wide range of benefits it proposes. These kinds of statistics are very easy to understand and attach to if I were to show them in my own website.

“Public Transportation System: Introduction or Expansion.” Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 26 Feb. 2024,
 archive.cdc.gov/www_cdc_gov/policy/hi5/publictransportation/index.html.

 The Center of Disease Control and Prevention's website on public transportation is a compilation of a lot of sources of information about the effects of public transit. It introduces the problem of using personal motor transport and how it affects the individual's physical health. It then brings evidence that supports the claim that public transportation mitigates these adverse effects and deaths. This source gives a lot more depth into the problems an individual might encounter without public transport, so it will be helpful for the areas of my website where I show a narrative of the negative effects of personal motor vehicles versus public transport.

“Smog Vehicle Emissions.” EPA, Environmental Protection Agency, 30 Mar. 2026, www.epa.gov/greenvehicles/smog-vehicle-emissions.

 The United States Environmental Protection Agency's website on smog vehicle emissions is a lengthy and detailed informational advisory on how much emissions are produced by different means such as personal motor vehicles. It also gives insight into how much emissions transport produces altogether compared to other modes of pollution. The EPA aims to lower emissions by the enactment of laws and regulations on motor vehicles. This website will give me the numbers to compare the emissions of cars versus trains specifically and can give me real numbers to show when considering transportation emissions as a whole.
